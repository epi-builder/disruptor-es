### Pitfall 2: Promoting smoke success directly into final ceiling classification

**What goes wrong:** A short smoke repro is used to claim the dominant sustained throughput ceiling is known. [VERIFIED: crates/app/src/http_stress.rs] [VERIFIED: docs/stress-results.md]
**Why it happens:** Smoke proves scrape health and report semantics, but the roadmap requires regenerated baseline lane evidence for final closure. [VERIFIED: .planning/ROADMAP.md] [VERIFIED: scripts/compare-stress-layers.sh]
**How to avoid:** Use smoke only as the gate to decide whether full baseline regeneration is likely to produce authoritative observed fields. [VERIFIED: docs/stress-results.md]
**Warning signs:** The summary cites only `--profile smoke` or only direct `cargo run ... --measure-seconds 2` outputs for the final ceiling label. [VERIFIED: docs/stress-results.md]

### Pitfall 3: Reading null scrape-derived fields as if they were zeros

**What goes wrong:** `append_latency_p95_micros = null` or `shard_depth_max = null` is treated as “no latency” or “no depth.” [VERIFIED: target/phase-13.1/layer-comparison/live-http-unique.json] [VERIFIED: crates/app/src/stress.rs]
**Why it happens:** The report contract intentionally models missing observed values as `Option` fields rather than synthetic measurements. [VERIFIED: crates/app/src/stress.rs] [VERIFIED: .planning/phases/13.1-disruptor-throughput-bottleneck-investigation-and-runtime-st/13.1-05-SUMMARY.md]
**How to avoid:** Require successful scrapes in the cited artifacts and quote missing data as missing, not zero. [VERIFIED: docs/stress-results.md]
**Warning signs:** Any bottleneck summary that explains `null` fields numerically or omits `metrics_scrape_successes` entirely. [VERIFIED: docs/stress-results.md]
